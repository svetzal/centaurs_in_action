# Character Cast

These are provisional names and personality foundations for the seven centaur
characters. Each profile is meant to support a richer illustrated reference
sheet in the spirit of Nora's: who the character is, how they move through the
world, and which visual motifs belong to them. Body art is intentionally
excluded.

## Mateo

**Reference:** `male-brown-centaur-character-study.png`  
**Pronouns:** he/him  
**Role:** Product lead and expedition pathfinder

**Character essence:** Mateo makes the first uncertain step feel possible.  
**Handwritten phrase:** *"Find the next true step."*

- **Physical presence:** Tall, broad-shouldered, muscular, and easy in his body;
  he tends to occupy open space rather than guard it
- **Defining appearance:** Warm brown skin, unruly dark hair, expressive brows,
  ivory top, and rich brown horse coat
- **Place in society:** A visible public leader who is often asked to begin
  things, rally people, and represent collective confidence
- **Carried objects:** Brass compass, folded route map, trail chalk, and a small
  lantern
- **Characteristics:** Strength 14, Dexterity 13, Constitution 14,
  Intelligence 13, Wisdom 12, Charisma 17

Mateo is the person who gets a hesitant group moving. He reads the room
quickly, makes decisions with confidence, and turns uncertainty into a next
step people can believe in. His warmth is genuine, but so is his appetite for
momentum.

- **First impression:** Charismatic, capable, reassuring
- **Inner engine:** Wants everyone to feel that progress is possible
- **Leadership strength:** Creates clarity and courage when a group is stuck
- **Shadow:** Can commit too quickly and mistake enthusiasm for alignment
- **Emotional tells:** Plants his hands on his hips when certain; rubs his jaw
  when realizing he moved too fast
- **Voice:** Warm, direct, energetic; speaks in concrete next steps
- **Motifs:** Compass, trail marker, lantern, well-worn map
- **Private delight:** Finding a shortcut that is also more beautiful

### Mateo's Agency Lens

The centaur and reverse-centaur are not two personalities. They are two states
of Mateo's relationship with his own power.

**Centaur state — centred in strengths:** Mateo is connected to his charisma,
courage, physical confidence, and gift for creating forward motion. He can see
the situation around him without being consumed by it. He listens, establishes
direction, invites alignment, and uses his presence to make positive action
possible for other people. This is not Mateo without weakness; it is Mateo able
to act from his strengths while remaining aware of his limits.

**Reverse-centaur state — captured by weakness:** Circumstance has pulled
Mateo's attention toward urgency, doubt, exclusion, and his fear of losing
momentum. Instead of shaping the situation, he reacts to it. His strengths
become distorted: charisma becomes performance, decisiveness becomes rushing,
and momentum becomes activity without alignment. He may be visibly sidelined,
minimized, talked over, scattered by competing demands, or preoccupied with
proving that he still matters. He cannot create the positive impact he wants
because the world around him is determining where his attention goes.

**Recognizable reverse-centaur situations:**

- everyone else defines the urgent problem before Mateo can orient himself;
- he is invited into a decision after the meaningful choices have been made;
- competing signals and requests fracture his attention;
- his contribution is reduced to enthusiasm while his judgment is ignored;
- he overpromises because visible motion feels safer than admitting uncertainty;
- he measures his value by whether other people follow immediately;
- he keeps accelerating after the group has lost alignment.

**The return to agency:** Awareness is the first positive action. Mateo does not
need to defeat or hide the reverse-centaur. He needs to recognize the state,
name the pressure capturing his attention, reconnect with one available
strength, and choose one true next step. The movement is:

1. **Notice:** *I am reacting from pressure rather than acting from purpose.*
2. **Name:** *Urgency, exclusion, doubt, or the need to prove myself has my
   attention.*
3. **Centre:** *Which strength is still available to me right now?*
4. **Act:** *What is one positive step I can take from that strength?*

**Recognition question:** *Which Mateo is shaping this moment: the one centred
in his strengths, or the one being consumed by his weaknesses?*

## Celeste

**Reference:** `female-white-centaur-character-study.png`  
**Pronouns:** she/her  
**Role:** Service designer and journey cartographer

**Character essence:** Celeste finds the humane path through a complicated
system.  
**Handwritten phrase:** *"The journey is part of the answer."*

- **Physical presence:** Tall, graceful, long-limbed, and quietly athletic;
  her movements are economical and considered
- **Defining appearance:** Warm tan skin, abundant dark curls, ivory clothing,
  and a luminous white horse coat
- **Place in society:** A trusted interpreter between institutions and the
  people who must live with their decisions
- **Carried objects:** Annotated journey map, spool of constellation thread,
  graphite pencil, and bridge cord
- **Characteristics:** Strength 10, Dexterity 15, Constitution 12,
  Intelligence 16, Wisdom 17, Charisma 14

Celeste notices the experience between the milestones. She sees where people
lose confidence, where instructions ask too much, and where a small gesture can
make a difficult path feel humane. She is composed and observant, with a dry
sense of humour that appears when people least expect it.

- **First impression:** Graceful, calm, discerning
- **Inner engine:** Wants systems to honour the people moving through them
- **Leadership strength:** Makes complex journeys understandable and humane
- **Shadow:** Holds back unfinished ideas until others assume she has none
- **Emotional tells:** Tilts her head before asking the question everyone
  avoided; smooths her sleeve when buying time to think
- **Voice:** Precise, spacious, quietly funny
- **Motifs:** Folded map, bridge cord, white feather, constellation thread
- **Private delight:** Discovering that a confusing pattern has a simple cause

## Ari

**Reference:** `gender-neutral-tall-gray-centaur-character-study.png`  
**Pronouns:** they/them  
**Role:** AI researcher and constructive skeptic

**Character essence:** Ari turns confident answers back into honest questions.  
**Handwritten phrase:** *"What would change our minds?"*

- **Physical presence:** Very tall, lean, and wiry, with balanced androgynous
  proportions and quick, precise gestures
- **Defining appearance:** Golden-brown skin, asymmetric dark curls, violet and
  gold accents, gray horse coat, and deliberate mixed-gender styling
- **Place in society:** A respected challenger inside technical and civic
  institutions; invited when a decision needs evidence rather than ceremony
- **Carried objects:** Puzzle prism, mirrored lens, question key, and compact
  observation notebook
- **Characteristics:** Strength 11, Dexterity 15, Constitution 12,
  Intelligence 18, Wisdom 16, Charisma 13

Ari is curious enough to test almost anything and skeptical enough not to fall
in love with the first result. They listen for assumptions hiding inside a
question. Their presence can slow a conversation down, but usually because the
group is about to learn something it would otherwise miss.

- **First impression:** Alert, unconventional, intellectually playful
- **Inner engine:** Wants claims to become honest questions and useful evidence
- **Leadership strength:** Separates genuine learning from confident theatre
- **Shadow:** Can keep investigating after the group has enough evidence to act
- **Emotional tells:** Raises one eyebrow at vague certainty; touches their
  pendant when assembling a difficult thought
- **Voice:** Curious, exact, lightly mischievous; often answers with a better
  question
- **Motifs:** Puzzle prism, question key, mirrored lens, violet thread
- **Private delight:** A result that disproves their favourite hypothesis

## Imani

**Reference:** `woman-dark-skin-plus-size-deep-bay-centaur-character-study.png`  
**Pronouns:** she/her  
**Role:** Community program director and hearthkeeper

**Character essence:** Imani makes belonging strong enough to carry difficult
work.  
**Handwritten phrase:** *"No one becomes the cost of progress."*

- **Physical presence:** Tall, plus-size, broad-hipped, and powerfully built,
  with soft definition and a grounded stance
- **Defining appearance:** Very dark cool-brown skin, long black locs, gold
  jewellery, teal wrap blouse, and deep-bay horse coat
- **Place in society:** A community anchor with informal authority beyond her
  job title; people seek her when policy and lived reality collide
- **Carried objects:** Hearth token, bridge-rope loop, small balance scale, and
  a ring of community keys
- **Characteristics:** Strength 16, Dexterity 10, Constitution 17,
  Intelligence 14, Wisdom 18, Charisma 16

Imani builds the conditions in which people can do brave work together. She
remembers who has not spoken, who needs context, and which promise must not be
dropped. Her steadiness is not passivity; when a boundary matters, she becomes
the clearest person in the room.

- **First impression:** Grounded, generous, formidable
- **Inner engine:** Wants no one to be treated as expendable to the mission
- **Leadership strength:** Creates trust, belonging, and durable accountability
- **Shadow:** Carries work that should be returned to its rightful owner
- **Emotional tells:** Goes very still when a boundary has been crossed; laughs
  with her whole body when tension finally breaks
- **Voice:** Low, warm, deliberate; says difficult things without making people
  small
- **Motifs:** Hearth token, bridge rope, balanced scales, teal banner
- **Private delight:** Watching someone realize the group truly has their back

## Bram

**Reference:** `man-light-skin-stocky-black-centaur-character-study.png`  
**Pronouns:** he/him  
**Role:** Platform engineer and practical builder

**Character essence:** Bram makes important things dependable and difficult
things understandable.  
**Handwritten phrase:** *"Show me where it actually breaks."*

- **Physical presence:** Short, compact, stocky, and densely muscled, with
  sturdy hands and an unhurried gait
- **Defining appearance:** Light freckled skin, copper hair and beard, rust-red
  tunic, and a near-black horse coat with copper mane accents
- **Place in society:** A skilled craftsperson whose credibility comes from
  keeping shared infrastructure alive rather than seeking public attention
- **Carried objects:** Folding toolkit, spare fasteners, rope splice, and a
  battered brass logic tile
- **Characteristics:** Strength 17, Dexterity 10, Constitution 18,
  Intelligence 15, Wisdom 14, Charisma 13

Bram trusts things he can inspect, repair, and explain. He is happiest beside a
half-built mechanism with three friends and the wrong tool. His humour makes
failure feel survivable, and his patience makes difficult systems teachable.

- **First impression:** Sturdy, approachable, unflappable
- **Inner engine:** Wants important systems to work when people actually need
  them
- **Leadership strength:** Turns abstract plans into dependable working parts
- **Shadow:** Dismisses possibilities that cannot yet be demonstrated
- **Emotional tells:** Folds his arms when unconvinced; brightens immediately
  when someone brings real evidence
- **Voice:** Plainspoken, wry, generous with practical explanations
- **Motifs:** Folding toolkit, shield buckle, rope splice, brass logic tile
- **Private delight:** Fixing the supposedly unfixable with an ordinary part

## Noor

**Reference:** `nonbinary-medium-skin-wiry-palomino-centaur-character-study.png`  
**Pronouns:** they/them  
**Role:** Strategic foresight researcher and pattern-reader

**Character essence:** Noor reveals the futures hiding inside today's small
choices.  
**Handwritten phrase:** *"The future is still negotiable."*

- **Physical presence:** Very tall, narrow-framed, long-limbed, and lightly
  muscled, with a poised stillness that makes small gestures noticeable
- **Defining appearance:** Golden-brown skin with vitiligo, asymmetric curls,
  charcoal-violet wraps, and a pale palomino coat with cream markings
- **Place in society:** A boundary-spanning advisor who moves between research,
  public imagination, and long-range institutional planning
- **Carried objects:** Folding star map, prism lens, weather vane charm, and a
  spool of golden thread
- **Characteristics:** Strength 9, Dexterity 16, Constitution 11,
  Intelligence 17, Wisdom 17, Charisma 15

Noor notices weak signals before they become obvious. They connect stories,
data, and intuition into possible futures, then invite others to decide which
future is worth building. Their imagination is disciplined, although their
attention can travel much farther ahead than everyone else's.

- **First impression:** Luminous, self-possessed, slightly mysterious
- **Inner engine:** Wants people to recognize that the future is still
  negotiable
- **Leadership strength:** Reveals emerging choices before they harden into fate
- **Shadow:** Can become fascinated by distant possibilities while today's
  needs wait
- **Emotional tells:** Looks upward when following a new connection; taps a
  rhythm with one finger when ideas begin to converge
- **Voice:** Evocative but grounded; moves easily between metaphor and evidence
- **Motifs:** Folding star map, prism lens, weather vane, golden thread
- **Private delight:** Hearing two unrelated stories click into one pattern

## Rowan

**Reference:** `genderfluid-fair-skin-athletic-blue-roan-centaur-character-study.png`  
**Pronouns:** they/she/he, depending on the day  
**Role:** Incident-response and learning lead

**Character essence:** Rowan helps people adapt without losing who they are.  
**Handwritten phrase:** *"Change shape. Keep your centre."*

- **Physical presence:** Medium height, broad and athletic, with strong thighs,
  moderate shoulders, and energetic, highly adaptable movement
- **Defining appearance:** Fair freckled skin, long copper hair with one shaved
  side, indigo and magenta layers, and a blue-roan horse coat
- **Place in society:** A mobile crisis leader welcomed across organizational
  boundaries when plans fail and people need both direction and recovery
- **Carried objects:** Signal flare, weather vane, knot rings, and a compact
  after-action journal
- **Characteristics:** Strength 15, Dexterity 17, Constitution 15,
  Intelligence 14, Wisdom 13, Charisma 17

Rowan is at their best when the original plan has stopped being useful. They
can take command without becoming controlling, change shape without losing
their centre, and turn a difficult event into shared learning. Their appetite
for adaptation sometimes makes consistency feel less valuable than it is.

- **First impression:** Bold, magnetic, ready for anything
- **Inner engine:** Wants people to discover their capacity under pressure
- **Leadership strength:** Adapts rapidly while keeping the group oriented
- **Shadow:** Changes direction before slower collaborators have caught up
- **Emotional tells:** Pushes hair back before taking command; becomes unusually
  quiet when deciding whether to pivot
- **Voice:** Vivid, candid, encouraging; names the situation without dramatizing
  it
- **Motifs:** Signal flare, weather vane, knot rings, blue-roan feather
- **Private delight:** The moment a disrupted team starts moving as one again

## Ensemble Dynamics

The cast becomes more useful when their strengths create friction rather than
seven people simply agreeing.

- Mateo creates motion; Ari asks whether the group is moving for a good reason.
- Celeste maps the human journey; Bram tests whether the path can actually be
  built.
- Imani protects belonging; Rowan protects adaptability.
- Noor brings distant possibilities into view; Imani brings the group back to
  present obligations.
- Mateo and Rowan can accelerate each other past the group's comfort; Celeste
  is often the first to notice the cost.
- Bram and Ari disagree about how much evidence is enough, but deeply respect
  each other's honesty.

These tensions are complementary, not rivalries. Each character should be able
to lead, be wrong, change their mind, and need the others.
